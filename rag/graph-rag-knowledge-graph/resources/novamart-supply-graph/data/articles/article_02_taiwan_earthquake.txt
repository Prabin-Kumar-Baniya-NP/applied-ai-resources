Magnitude 6.2 Earthquake Strikes Near Taipei, Chip Fabs Assess Damage

TAIPEI — A magnitude 6.2 earthquake struck near Taipei early this morning, prompting
precautionary shutdowns at multiple semiconductor fabrication facilities. While no major
structural damage has been confirmed, memory and storage chip producers in the Taipei area
have paused output to inspect equipment.

Industry sources expect a moderate disruption lasting several days as fabs recalibrate
sensitive manufacturing tools. Storage and memory module supply may tighten in the short term.
